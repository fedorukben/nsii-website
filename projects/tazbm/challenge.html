The SenseHB Network: Creating a Zero-Barrier Air Travel for People with Sensory Processing Disorder
Accessible Mobility: Towards a Zero-Barrier Mobility Online Challenge

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that 1.1 billion people (14.1% of the world's population) suffer from some form of disability. This represents the world's largest minority and the only minority group that any of us can become a member of at any time. Population aging is also an increasing phenomenon due to declining fertility rates and rising life expectancy. According to UN's World Population Prospects Report, more than 20% of Canadians will be over the age 85 by 2025. Aging usually results in losing various faculties such as memory, eyesight, hearing, and mobility.

Accessible mobility aims at facilitating the mobility of physically challenged individuals and seniors. Accessible mobility enables these people to get from one place to another safely, independently, confidently, and seamlessly. The objective of this competition is twofold: (1) to raise public awareness ofthe mobility barriers that face physically challenged individuals and seniors in their daily life and (2) to motivate students and researchers to identify and work on innovative user-centric solutions, which can empower these people and handle their mobility barriers.

Several accessible mobility solutions are developed to facilitate the mobility of physically challenged individuals and seniors. These solutions include, but are not limited to, ergonomics and mobility aids, motability Scheme, exoskeletons, soft exosuits, autonomous wheelchairs, voice-enabled navigation apps, vision and speech aids, tracking devices, healthcare transportation and accessibility maps, to name just a few. However, without a user-driven approach, there is a danger that ill-conceived technologies will at best be irrelevant or inappropriate and at worst will reinforce some of the negative ageist assumptions that frame much of society's response to disability and/or aging. This user-driven approach can drive the specification, development, and deployment of the accessible mobility service/system to have practical benefits for users in their everyday lives.
